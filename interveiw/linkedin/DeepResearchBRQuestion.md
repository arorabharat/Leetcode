Why do you want to work at LinkedIn, and why are you interested in this role? – The interviewer is looking for alignment with LinkedIn’s mission/values and how your background fits the role.

Tell me about a time when you worked closely with cross-functional teams (e.g. product managers, design, analytics). How did you collaborate effectively? – Expect demonstration of communication, adaptability, and teamwork across disciplines.

Describe a situation where you had to handle ambiguous requirements and prioritize tasks. How did you approach it? – They want to see how you handle uncertainty and make decisions (using data or intuition) under vague conditions.

Tell me about a time you faced a conflict of ideas with a teammate. How did you handle the disagreement? – Look for conflict-resolution skills, listening to others, and finding consensus or compromise.

Give an example of a time when one of your ideas or proposals was not accepted by your team. How did you respond? – This probes your humility and teamwork: did you adapt or learn from the feedback?.

Describe a project whose outcomes did not meet your expectations. What happened, and what did you learn? – Tests how you handle setbacks, problem-solving under failure, and lessons learned from it.

Tell me about a time you delivered results despite limited resources (time, people, budget). How did you succeed? – The interviewer is looking for resourcefulness, prioritization, and ownership in tough conditions.

Describe a time when you realized you lacked an important skill on a project. How did you address it? – Focus on self-awareness and learning: did you acquire the skill, seek help, or adjust your approach?.

What are your expectations for this role? – They want to gauge your goals and whether they match the team’s work and culture.

What kind of projects or work are you most excited to do in this role? – This reveals your interests and how they align with the team’s mission and LinkedIn’s products
